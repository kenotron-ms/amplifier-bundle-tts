    # AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — "Chroma After Midnight"
    ## The Kitchen Scene

    ## THE SCENE: Chase & Lia's Apartment, Chroma District, 2 AM
    A narrow apartment stacked high in a megabuilding. Neon advertisements press
    against the windows like uninvited guests — red, blue, violet. The city never
    goes quiet; neither does the worry that lives here. Chase has just come home
    carrying 5,000 credits and something he won't name. Lia is waiting.

    ### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
    Style: Documentary warmth over a cold subject. The narrator speaks with the
    quiet intimacy of someone who already knows how this story ends — not grim,
    just honest. The dialogue that follows should feel worn-in, like a conversation
    that's been rehearsed in each person's head for months.
    Pacing: Unhurried. Pauses carry weight.
    Accent: Neutral mid-Atlantic — clear, no regional edge.

    ### SAMPLE CONTEXT
    This is the first scene of the story. We are being introduced to Chase —
    a man who kills for money and calls it providing. Lia knows. The city hums
    outside. A child sleeps down the hall.

    #### TRANSCRIPT
    [warmly] Some nights the neon city almost looks beautiful from up here.
    Chase came home late, as he always did, and set a stack of credits on the table
    without a word — five thousand of them, crisp and uncomplicated.
    Lia took the money. She always took the money.
    But tonight was different.

    Lia: [quietly] I do wish you'd tell me how you get all this money, Chase.
    Chase: Does it matter? I'm providing for you and Jack, aren't I?
    Lia: It does matter. I don't want you to put yourself in danger for us.
    Chase: What's the alternative? You start working? I work hard so that you can have the life you want. What I want doesn't matter.
    Lia: I want you to have the life you want too, Chase.
    Chase: [flat] My job pays well. I'm not leaving it.
    Lia: [serious] I'm not dumb, Chase. I know you've been working for the Scavs.

    [pause — the city hums outside]

    Chase: [sighs] It's a little late for me to stop now, Lia.
    Lia: It's never too late.
    Chase: [tired] I've killed people, Lia.
    Lia: I know. I've seen the blood on your clothes.
    Chase: [serious] I've killed powerful people. You can't just erase that.
    Lia: So we'll move. We can leave Chroma and go to some other district. Helios is a big city.
    Chase: [quietly] You really think so?
    Lia: I do. We can even start looking right now if you'd like.
    Chase: What will we do? For work, I mean.
    Lia: We'll figure it out. Corpo's always hiring — you could use your expertise there.
    Chase: [sighs] Maybe this is a conversation for tomorrow. I'll stay home to figure it out.
    Lia: [warmly] Thank you. That means a lot to me.

    [a hug, in the neon-lit kitchen — the city outside still dangerous, still beautiful]
