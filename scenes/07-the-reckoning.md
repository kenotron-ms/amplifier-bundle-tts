# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — "The Door"
## The Reckoning Scene

## THE SCENE: Chase's Apartment, Later That Night — Then the Dining Table
Chase cannot sleep. He stands at the window with a glass of water, watching
the neon signs blink their indifferent colours across the sky, half-asleep,
thinking about leaving the Scavs, about tomorrow, about Jack. Then someone
starts pounding on the door. A voice Chase recognizes — even through the door —
as someone who has already decided how this ends.

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
Style: Taut and firm. No warmth. The narrator delivers this the way a judge
reads a verdict — not without feeling, but without sentimentality. The tension
in the scene is the gap between what we know is coming and what these characters
don't know yet. Jack's presence throughout — the child at the corner, kicking
his legs against the chair — is the thing that makes it unbearable.
Pacing: Controlled throughout. Then — when it happens — quick.
Accent: American. Clipped. Final-sounding.

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze has found his daughter's body. He has the Daemon's address from the datachip.
He has come to the apartment. Chase knows who Blaze is the moment he hears the voice.
Lia opens the door anyway — there is no rejecting a voice like that.
Jack has snuck out to watch. He doesn't understand what he's about to see.

#### TRANSCRIPT
[firm] Chase lay in bed next to Lia and could not sleep.
Everything was too hot under the blankets and too cold without them.
He got up. Put on casualwear. Made his way to the kitchen.
Filled a glass of water and stood at the window.

The city was the same as it always was —
neon signs, floating advertisements, the occasional sound of something
that wasn't fireworks.

[pause]

He almost dropped the glass when the pounding started.

[controlled] Through the door came a voice.
Cold. Certain. Already in the room, somehow, before the door had opened.

Don't do this, Daemon.

[a beat]

Chase said he had no idea who they were talking about.
He was a very bad liar, even then.

[pause]

Jack was watching from the hallway corner,
pink stuffed animal pressed to his chest.
His mother joined Chase at the door.
She opened it.

[firm] The man at the door was Blaze.
He sat at the head of the table as though he owned it.

You have a kid too? he said, looking at Jack.
You've been busy.

[pause — the sound of a chair]

At least let me put the kid to sleep, Chase said.

No. The kid stays.

[long pause — the table between them, the stuffed animal on the table between them]

[slower] Blaze's eyes moved across the room and landed on the pink dog.

[quietly] That's a pretty stuffed animal. Could I see it?

Sure thing! Jack said. My dad brought it for me from work!

[a pause that lasts too long]

[barely contained] Blaze turned to Chase.

You monster.

[firm, rapid] He recognized that animal from anywhere.
He had given it to his daughter on her third birthday.
He remembered her face.
Then he remembered the chamber.

[a gunshot. then silence. then another.]

[very still] When it was over, Blaze holstered his weapon.
He looked at Jack.
Jack's face had become something unrecognizable.

[pause]

Images of his daughter in his mind.

[firm, final] He holstered the gun.

Come with me, boy.

[pause]

Welcome to your new family.
