# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The End of Everything”
## “Come with me, boy, welcome to your new family.”

## THE SCENE: The living room — the table, the gun, the aftermath
The table. A gun drawn in one smooth motion. A wife who tried to run. A man who threw noodles. A boy still holding a pink stuffed animal with one ear missing. Then: silence. Blaze standing in the living room of a family he has destroyed, looking at the only one left standing.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Devastating and level — the narrator delivers the killings with the same flat register as everything else; do not cry, do not shake; the evenness is the horror
* “I’ll be better than him, he thought.” — brief, genuine; Blaze’s only moment of self-reflection; it does not redeem him; it does not need to
Pacing: The gun sequence is fast — practiced violence; the final line is slow and quiet; there is no music; the sentence ends the story
Accent: Neutral, final

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze recognized the stuffed animal. Now the room pays. Lia goes first. Chase goes after. The boy watches both. Blaze looks at the boy and sees his daughter’s face. He holsters the gun. He has chosen, in his way, not to be what the villain in Chase’s bedtime story was — a father who does not love his child. What he chooses instead is its own kind of horror.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Blaze turned towards the Daemon. “You monster,” he hissed. Blaze recognized that stuffed animal from anywhere. It was his daughter’s. He remembered giving it to her on her 3rd birthday, he remembered the pure joy on her face, and then he remembered her mutilated form of pure terror.

Pure anger flooded his senses, intoxicating him nearly as well as a good drink. He stood from the table and drew his handgun in one smooth motion, pointing it at the Daemon. The wife screamed, attempting to run away before he turned and put a bullet in her head.

The Daemon threw a cup of noodles at Blaze, but he easily swatted it away. He emptied the rest of his mag into the Daemon, killing him. He turned towards the boy, staring him down. The boy’s face contorted into sadness and terror. Images of his daughter flashed in his mind. He holstered the gun. I’ll be better than him, he thought.

“Come with me, boy, welcome to your new family.”
