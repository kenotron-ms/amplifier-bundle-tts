# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Stuffed Animal”
## “Rage.”

## THE SCENE: The living room table — Blaze at the head, Chase and Lia opposite, Jack between them
The apartment feels smaller than it is. The table fills the center. Blaze sits at the head as if he has always owned this room. The stuffed animal sits on the table between them—pink, one ear missing. Blaze scans the room. His eyes find it.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Taut and inevitable — the narrator tracks Blaze’s gaze as it travels the room and finds the stuffed animal; the reader must feel recognition forming before “Rage.” arrives
* “Rage.” — single word, single period, complete stop; do not rush toward it or away from it; hold the silence after
Pacing: Slow scan of the room, tightening to absolute stillness; then the word lands
Accent: Neutral, grave

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze is holding everything together through compression — blood boiling so hot he can barely sit still. He scans the room for something to confirm what he already knows. He finds it on the table. He gave his daughter that stuffed animal on her third birthday. He watched the pure joy on her face. It is here, brought home “from work” by the man who took everything.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Blaze sat across the table from the three. Blood boiling so hot he could barely hold himself back from killing all three of them right there. He scanned the room, glancing around before his eyes landed on the stuffed animal on the table.

“That’s a pretty stuffed animal, could I see it?” he said, reaching towards the boy.

“Sure thing! My dad brought it for me from work!”

Rage.
