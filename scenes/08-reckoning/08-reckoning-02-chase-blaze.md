# AUDIO PROFILE: Chase + Blaze — “The Door”
## “No, the kid stays.”

## THE SCENE: The Chroma apartment front door, then the living room table, deep night
The front door stands open. Blaze fills the frame. Chase faces him. Behind the corner, Lia and Jack wait. The living room table becomes a tribunal—Chase and Lia on one side, Jack between them, Blaze at the head. The city hums indifferently outside.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Chase (Algenib, Gravelly): controlled denial giving way to barely-contained desperation; trying to hold the situation closed with his hands
* Blaze (Alnilam, Firm): unhurried and completely in control — he has already won; he is enjoying the architecture of the moment; “No, the kid stays” is soft and total
Pacing: The door exchange is taut and clipped; the table scene settles into something worse — cold social ritual with no exits
Accent: Blaze — tight authority; Chase — working-city, increasingly strained

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze has traced the Daemon to his front door. Chase is denying everything. Lia steps out because there is no rejecting a voice like that. Blaze sits at the head of the table and takes in the whole picture — the wife, the apartment, the boy. [Lia, gently:] "“I’m Lia, and who are you? And why are you here in the middle of the night?”" — two lines; she disappears into the scene after. When Chase asks to put Jack to bed, the denial is quiet and absolute.

#### TRANSCRIPT
“Who’s your father talking to?” His mother whispered to him.

“I don’t know.”

Lia motioned for Jack to stay behind the corner as she stepped out to join Chase at the door.

“Well well well, you’re married?” The voice said from behind the door.

[Lia, gently:] “I’m Lia, and who are you? And why are you here in the middle of the night?”

“I’m just a friend of Chase’s… from work,” he paused, “You know you really should let me in, y’know, manners and stuff.”

Lia’s face was white as a sheet. There was no rejecting a voice like that. The front door creaked as it opened, heavy footsteps signalling the visitor. Jack moved over to the table, getting ready for the conversation ahead. He wasn’t about to let his parents have all the fun. He set his stuffed animal on the table next to him, kicking his legs.

“You have a kid too?” the visitor said, turning towards Jack, “You’ve been busy.” He took a seat at the head of the table.

Lia and Chase sat at the other end of the table with Jack.

“At least let me put the kid to sleep, Blaze,” Chase said, motioning towards Jack.

“No, the kid stays.”
