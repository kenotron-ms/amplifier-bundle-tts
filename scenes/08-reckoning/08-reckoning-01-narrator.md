# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Boy at the Corner”
## “I can always tell when dad’s lying.”

## THE SCENE: The Chroma apartment, Jack’s bedroom and front hallway, deep night
Jack’s dark room. Then the front hallway. Whispers travel through the apartment walls. Jack gets dressed and creeps to the corner, pink stuffed animal pressed to his chest, peeking around the edge. He can see his father at the door. He can hear a voice from outside. He can already tell his dad is lying.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Taut and child-focused — the narrator sees through Jack’s eyes; innocence makes the dread heavier
* “I can always tell when dad’s lying, he’s such a bad liar” is Jack’s interior voice — deliver with a child’s simple certainty, no irony
Pacing: Quiet and careful; Jack moves in slow increments; the stakes arrive through his observations, not through action
Accent: Neutral, slightly warmer register for the child’s POV

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
The pounding on the door has woken the apartment. Chase is at the door doing damage control — denying he knows Blaze’s daughter. Jack can hear everything and knows his dad is lying. He sneaks out with his stuffed animal just as his mother wakes and joins him in the hall.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Jack woke up to the sounds of loud whispers coming from the front door. Dad? Who was the other voice? He got dressed and slowly snuck out into the living room, holding his pink plush close to his chest while peeking around the corner of the front hallway to see his dad.

“I swear I’ve never seen her before!” His dad said. Why was he lying?

“Don’t do this Daemon,” a cold voice said from behind the door. Who is that?

“I have no idea who that is!” I can always tell when dad’s lying, he’s such a bad liar. Maybe I should help him.

Around this time, he heard footsteps down the bedroom hallway. His mom had woken up.
