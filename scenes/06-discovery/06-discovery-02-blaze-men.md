# AUDIO PROFILE: Blaze + Henchman — “The Room”
## “Blood, smells like it.”

## THE SCENE: South Atlantic megabuilding, lower-level corridor and the room, night
The hallway from the security footage made real — damp concrete, a long corridor, a door at the end. Inside: a plain sparse room, muted furniture, a bathroom. The smell hits before the lights come up fully. Blaze moves through it like a doctor performing a diagnosis.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Blaze (Alnilam, Firm): moves through the room like a blade — every observation terse and certain; “No, this is different, more of it” is flat and final
* Henchman (Charon, Informative): provides information with care; slight hesitation on “we already searched the room” — he knows this claim is about to fail
Pacing: Short, functional sentences; the room is being processed as evidence; no wasted breath
Accent: Blaze — tight authority; Henchman — measured, professional

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze steps into the room where his daughter was. His men believe they searched it completely. He already knows they missed something. The smell of blood — more than the cloth would explain — pulls him to the bathroom. What is behind the leftmost wall is what they were not looking hard enough to find.

#### TRANSCRIPT
“We grabbed that chip you saw,” one of them said, handing him a small metal chip. “We already went over it, and believe that it contains the location of the Daemon’s house.”

“Good, take me to that room, I would like to investigate it myself.”

“Of course.”

They led Blaze through the building, taking a short elevator ride down into the lower levels. After a walk down that hallway he remembered seeing in the footage, they arrived at the room. He stepped into the room, scanning it.

“Blood, smells like it,” Blaze said, smelling the air.

“We suspect that it could be from that cloth we found.”

“No, this is different, more of it.”

Blaze walked over to the bathroom. “More of it here.”

“With all due respect sir, we already searched the room.”
