# AUDIO PROFILE: Blaze + Henchman — “She Was Eight”
## “He’s all mine.”

## THE SCENE: The chamber in the megabuilding — immediately after the discovery, Blaze holding his daughter
The blast has cleared. The chamber is open. Blaze holds his daughter’s body. The men around him have gone still. The air smells of concrete dust and blood and something none of them have words for.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Blaze (Alnilam, Firm): the rage is inside the flatness — “She was only eight years old” in an eerily even tone is more frightening than shouting; “He’s all mine” is quiet and absolute
* Henchman (Charon, Informative): barely above a whisper; “How old was she?” was not meant to be heard; everything else is procedural compliance under extreme duress
Pacing: Near silence; every line is very short and very contained; understatement carries all the weight
Accent: Blaze — controlled and hollow; Henchman — subdued, careful

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
The worst thing has happened. Blaze’s men are witnesses to something they will not survive describing. One man whispers too loudly. Blaze answers him with a fact: eight years old. Then he gives the order that will end the Daemon’s life. “He’s all mine” means no one else touches the Daemon before Blaze does.

#### TRANSCRIPT
One of his men leaned in to one of the others, “How old was she?” he whispered.

“She was only eight years old,” Blaze said in an eerily flat tone. “You said you found his base?”

“Yes, should I tell the others?”

“Lock down the building but don’t mess with the Daemon.” Blaze said turning to leave, carrying his daughter’s corpse, “He’s all mine.”
