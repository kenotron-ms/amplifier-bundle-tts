# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Footage”
## “Nothing. Nothing. Something.”

## THE SCENE: Blaze’s car in transit, then the South Atlantic megabuilding entrance, night
A sleek black car sliding through rain-slicked Chroma streets. Blaze sits in the back; his vision flashes between the real world and low-quality security footage. A hallway. A door. The Daemon. A body carried like luggage.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Cold and precise — the narrator renders the footage with the flat affect of a surveillance report
* “Nothing. Nothing. Something.” — three beats; the third lands like a fist; give each its own pause
Pacing: Controlled with a surge during the footage review; settle to measured on arrival
Accent: Neutral, grave

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze has the datachip and the footage. In transit he reviews it: sees the Daemon carrying his daughter’s body, sees the door close on her. Then something falls from the Daemon’s jacket. A chip. A lead. He arrives at the building already knowing what he will find inside.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Blaze got in the sleek black car, motioning for the driver to head towards the megabuilding where they found the cloth. He sat back in his seat, downloading the footage from the security camera.

His vision flashed a couple times before swapping to the footage completely. Through the low-quality data, he saw a hallway leading to a lone door. Static. Droning. Then, a person. The Daemon. He was carrying Blaze’s daughter underneath his shoulder with her body hanging eerily lifeless as he opened the door and threw her inside. The footage ended there, or became useless at least. He rewound the footage, searching for any clues that could lead to the Daemon. Anything would be useful, hell he’d take a footprint. Nothing. Nothing. Something. A small object appeared to fall from the Daemon’s jacket as he threw Blaze’s daughter into the room. He blinked, dismissing the footage and returning his vision to the car.

Shortly after, they arrived at the building. His men stood outside the entrance, waiting for him to leave the car. He stepped out and greeted them.
