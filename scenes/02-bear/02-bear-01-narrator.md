# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Boy in the Hallway”
## “Are we in trouble?”

## THE SCENE: Chroma District apartment, front hallway, late night
The same apartment, minutes later. The kitchen light still burns at the far end of the hall. Jack pads out from the bedroom corridor in bare feet, rubbing sleep from his eyes. The argument Lia and Chase were having has gone quiet—but not quiet enough.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Warm and slightly surprised — the narrator’s register softens the moment Jack appears
* “Are we in trouble?” lands heavier than the boy knows; let the narrator’s warmth carry that irony gently
Pacing: Gentle; Jack moves slowly; everything is sleepy
Accent: Neutral, warm

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
The intensity of the kitchen conversation breaks when their son walks down the hall. Jack heard voices but doesn’t understand what he interrupted. He’s concerned about a missing stuffed bear — nothing more. Lia’s headshake tells Chase everything: no, there’s no bear. The gift Chase produces next will mean something much larger than either of them can say.

#### TRANSCRIPT
“Are we in trouble?” Jack said, walking down the hallway, rubbing his eyes.

“Jack, you should be asleep,” Chase said, walking over.

“But you and momma were too loud, and I lost my bear.”

Chase looked over to Lia. She shook her head.
