    # AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — "The Pink Dog"
    ## The Bear Scene

    ## THE SCENE: Apartment Hallway and Jack's Bedroom
    The apartment is settling into quiet after the hard conversation. Lia and Chase
    have pulled each other back from the edge. Then a small figure appears in the
    hallway in mismatched pajamas, rubbing his eyes. Jack. Six years old, maybe seven.
    He doesn't know what his father does. He only knows his dad brings things home from work.

    ### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
    Style: Warm and aching. This is the heart of the story's first act — a father
    who does terrible things, loving his son in the only way he knows how.
    The narrator holds both things at once without flinching from either.
    Pacing: Soft and unhurried. The child's dialogue should feel natural and half-asleep.
    Accent: Gentle — no hard edges anywhere.

    ### SAMPLE CONTEXT
    The conversation between Chase and Lia has just ended with a fragile kind of hope.
    Now Jack interrupts — confused, sleepy, missing his bear. Chase reaches into
    his bag and produces a pink stuffed animal with a missing ear. We learn later
    where it came from.

    #### TRANSCRIPT
    [softly] The kitchen light was still on when Jack padded down the hallway,
    clutching at the wall like it might run away.

    Are we in trouble? he asked — the way children ask,
    not knowing how accurate the question is.

    [warmly] Chase walked over and crouched down to his son's level.

    You should be asleep, buddy. What happened?

    But you and momma were too loud. And I lost my bear.

    Chase looked across the room at Lia. She shook her head — she didn't know where it was either.

    [gently] Aw, I'm sorry you lost your bear. But... I have a present for you.

    [pause — the sound of a bag being opened, rummaged through]

    [softly] From his bag, Chase produced a small pink stuffed animal.
    It was missing one ear. A dog, maybe — or something that used to be a dog.
    He handed it to Jack and patted him on the head.

    I found this for you. At work.

    [childlike wonder] Really?

    Yeah. Now — you said you couldn't sleep?

    [pause]

    Jack looked up at his father. He didn't say anything about the faint stain on the jacket sleeve.

    [quietly] ...Yes.

    Do you want me to tell you a story?

    [warmly] Jack nodded, and Chase led him back down the hallway,
    the city glowing softly behind them through the window.
