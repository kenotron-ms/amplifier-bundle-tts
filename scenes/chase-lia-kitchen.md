# AUDIO PROFILE: Chase & Lia
    ## "The Kitchen, Late Night"

    ## THE SCENE: Chroma District Apartment, 2 AM
    A cramped apartment high in a neon-drenched city tower. Through the windows, the dark skyline is
    cut by floating advertisements and occasional gunshots that nobody reacts to anymore. The kitchen
    light is the only thing on — harsh overhead fluorescent against the glow of a microwave.
    Chase has just come home from work. He hasn't told Lia what work means.

    ### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
    Style:
    - Chase: Controlled, defensive, quietly exhausted. He's had this fight before and he always wins
      by not fighting. Clipped. Final-sounding. But something underneath is cracking.
    - Lia: Measured resolve. She's calm because she decided to be calm — this is the conversation
      she's been rehearsing. She is not angry. She is scared and she loves him.
    Pacing: Slow. Deliberate pauses between lines. Neither of them is rushing this.
    Accent: Neutral urban American — no strong regional markers.

    ### SAMPLE CONTEXT
    Lia knows Chase works for the Scavs — a violent gang. She's known for a while.
    Tonight she finally said it out loud. Chase doesn't know how to stop being dangerous.
    The city outside is proof that this is normal. That makes it worse.

    #### TRANSCRIPT
    Lia: I do wish you'd tell me how you get all this money, Chase.
    Chase: Does it matter? I'm providing for you and Jack, aren't I?
    Lia: It does matter. I don't want you to put yourself in danger for us.
    Chase: What's the alternative? You start working? I work hard so that you can have the life you want. What I want doesn't matter.
    Lia: I want you to have the life you want too, Chase.
    Chase: My job pays well. I'm not leaving it.
    Lia: [serious] I'm not dumb, Chase. I know you've been working for the Scavs.
    Chase: [sighs] It's a little late for me to stop now, Lia.
    Lia: It's never too late.
    Chase: [tired] I've killed people, Lia.
    Lia: I know. I've seen the blood on your clothes.
    Chase: [serious] I've killed powerful people. You can't just erase that.
    Lia: So we'll move. We can leave Chroma and go to some other district. Helios is a big city.
    Chase: [quietly amazed] You really think so?
    Lia: I do. We can even start looking right now if you'd like.
    Chase: What will we do? For work, I mean.
    Lia: We'll figure it out. And I'm sure I can always find something.
    Chase: [sighs] Maybe this is a conversation for tomorrow. I'll stay home to figure it out.
    Lia: [warmly] Thank you. That means a lot to me.
