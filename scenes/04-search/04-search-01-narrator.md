# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Sweep”
## “Blaze’s gang split into three groups.”

## THE SCENE: South Atlantic District, Chroma — three megabuildings, night
Three colossal towers pressing up against a sky choked with neon. The Scavs own these blocks and the narrow alleys between them. Blaze moves with a team of six through the first building. Twenty more fan out through the others. Every door is a question with the same wrong answer.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Methodical and tactical — the narrator matches Blaze’s cold efficiency; no wasted words
* The building search feels inevitable; Blaze does not doubt he will find something, only where
Pacing: Spare and forward-moving; the sweep of the building is rendered in compressed, purposeful sentences
Accent: Neutral, controlled

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze has a district and three buildings. The Scavs who resist are dispatched without drama. The apartment Blaze kicks open at the end of this passage is the last one in the first building — empty, featureless, useless. But one of his men has found something in another building. The call is coming.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Blaze’s gang split into three groups, all searching different megabuildings. The Scavs owned South Atlantic, but Blaze and his men quickly dispatched the ones who tried anything stupid. Blaze joined a smaller group of 5 other men searching the first megabuilding. The remaining 20 were split searching the others.

Blaze kicked open a door, revealing a relatively plain apartment room. This should be the last one of the building. He wondered how the others were getting along with their buildings. His men rushed the room first, performing a sweep of the room before motioning to him that it was safe to enter. He entered, scanning the room for anything out of the ordinary before receiving a call from one of his men. With a nod he answered.
