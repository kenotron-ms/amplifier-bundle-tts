# AUDIO PROFILE: Blaze + Crum — “The Daughter”
## “You gonna’ tell me again how it is you lost my daughter?”

## THE SCENE: A concrete warehouse in lower Chroma, interrogation room, night
Bare concrete walls. A single overhead light. A raised platform at the back of the room. Crum is on his knees on the cold floor, sweat already running. Blaze stands above him, one finger on the trigger. The men along the walls don’t move.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Blaze (Alnilam, Firm): cold and absolute — no rage in his voice, only certainty; the terrifying calm of a man who has already decided; the trigger squeeze is academic
* Crum (Fenrir, Excitable): panicking, begging; voice breaks and accelerates; [panicked] throughout; “Please no!” is a shout that goes nowhere
Pacing: Blaze is glacial; Crum’s fear accelerates against his stillness; the gunshot ends it abruptly
Accent: Blaze — clipped authority; Crum — desperate working-city

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Crum is the man responsible for Blaze’s daughter going missing. There is no answer he can give that changes the outcome. The questions are theater for the men watching — a demonstration of what incompetence costs. The bullet is not a punishment; it is a conclusion.

#### TRANSCRIPT
“You gonna’ tell me again how it is you lost my daughter?” Blaze said, setting his finger on the trigger, the leather of his gloves lightly squeaking.

“I-it was the Daemon, sir,” [panicked] Crum said, his voice wavering. He made a point of not taking his eyes off the cool steel.

“I seem to remember you saying you could handle him,” Blaze said, lightly squeezing the trigger, stopping at the catch.

“Please no! We didn’t mean to!” Crum yelled.

Crum’s yelling was cut short as a bullet ripped through his skull, cyberware sparking as it tore through flesh and metal. His blood splattered the concrete floor behind him.
