# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “Too Hot, Too Cold”
## “His senses were dulled, which is why he nearly dropped the glass.”

## THE SCENE: The Chroma apartment, bedroom then kitchen, deep past midnight
Lia sleeps in the dark. Chase can’t. He sits up, moves through the apartment in casualwear, fills a glass from the kitchen tap. The window shows the city in its full neon insistence — floating signs, drifting advertisements, a sky that never rests. He stands there in a half-asleep daze. Then the door.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Restless and insomniac — the narrator carries the weight of Chase’s unresolved choice from the kitchen scene
* The description of the view is almost beautiful; this contrast with what’s about to happen should be held gently, not ironically
Pacing: Slow drift from bed to window; the pounding on the door breaks the rhythm sharply at the very end
Accent: Neutral, weighted

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Chase is lying in bed running the math on whether he can actually leave the Scavs. It’s a circular argument he can’t win at this hour. He gets up, goes to the kitchen, stands at the window with a glass of water. He is exactly the wrong kind of awake when Blaze finds the door.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Chase lay in his bed next to Lia. Everything was too hot under the blankets but too cold out of them. Could he really leave the Scavs? What would he do to provide for his family? He didn’t like his job with them but it beat typing away at a computer like a corpo. This was stupid. He sat up, making sure not to wake Lia. Maybe he was thirsty? He got out of bed and put on his casualwear, slowly making his way to the kitchen. He opened a cabinet and grabbed a glass from it, filling it up with the faucet in the sink. He took a sip of it as he padded over to the window. He wouldn’t miss the view, that’s for sure. Large neon signs and floating advertisements filled the sky outside, casting a harsh yet comforting glow into the living room. He stood there, looking out the window in a half-asleep daze. His senses were dulled, which is why he nearly dropped the glass when someone started pounding on the front door.
