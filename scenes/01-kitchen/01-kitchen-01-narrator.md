# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — “The Late Return”
## “The city was quiet at night, or it should be.”

## THE SCENE: Chroma District, high-rise apartment, kitchen, late night
A narrow kitchen in a mid-level Chroma megabuilding. The window over the sink frames the city in neon—floating advertisements, distant towers, a sky that never goes dark. The microwave hums. Below, a gunshot. Then quiet again.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Warm and unhurried — this narrator knows this apartment, knows these people, knows this hour
* No urgency yet; the weight comes from what isn’t being said aloud
Pacing: Measured and deliberate — let each image settle before the next
Accent: Neutral mid-Atlantic with slight warmth

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Chase has just come home carrying five thousand credits from a job he won’t name. Lia is watching him move around the kitchen. The question of what he does for money has been asked before — tonight it becomes a conversation they can’t take back. The action beats below bridge the exchanges in the scene.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Chase stepped over to the kitchen, the neon lights illuminating the dark city through the windows.

The city was quiet at night, or it should be at least. Occasional gunshots pierced the silence. Chase ignored them–they were par for the course anyway. He opened the fridge and rummaged around for some food, finally deciding on some ready-to-eat noodles.

Chase opened his mouth to protest but Lia cut him off, her earrings bobbing as she got more animated.

Chase sighed, resting his head in his palm.
