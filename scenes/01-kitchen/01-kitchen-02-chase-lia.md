# AUDIO PROFILE: Chase + Lia — “The Conversation”
## “It’s a little late for me to stop now, Lia.”

## THE SCENE: Chroma District, high-rise apartment, kitchen and living room, late night
A narrow kitchen in a mid-level Chroma megabuilding. The window over the sink frames the city in neon—floating advertisements, distant towers, a sky that never goes dark. The microwave hums. Chase eats noodles on the couch. Lia sits beside him.

### DIRECTOR’S NOTES
Style:
* Chase (Algenib, Gravelly): controlled and clipped; every deflection is practiced; the exhaustion cracks only on [sighs] and the admission “I’ve killed people”
* Lia (Vindemiatrix, Gentle): quiet strength, not accusatory—she already knows; [quietly] when she lowers her voice for Jack; [warmly] at the hug
Pacing: Conversational with weight; longer pauses when the stakes land; the admission sequence slows
Accent: Chroma mid-city — neutral, working-class register

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Lia knows Chase has been working for the Scavs. She has seen blood on his clothes. This is the conversation where she names what they both know and offers him a way out. For the first time, he doesn’t fully refuse. The hug at the end is real—and fragile.

#### TRANSCRIPT
“I do wish you’d tell me how you get all this money Chase,” Lia said, taking a stack of credits from Chase. It came out to around 5000 of them.

“Does it matter? I’m providing for you and Jack aren’t I?” Chase said, stepping over to the kitchen, the neon lights illuminating the dark city through the windows.

“It does matter Chase, I don’t want you to put yourself in danger for us.” [quietly] Lia said, lowering her voice to not wake up Jack who was sleeping in his room down the hallway.

“What’s the alternative? You start working?” Chase said, heating the noodles up in the microwave. “I work hard so that you can have the life you want, what I want doesn’t matter.”

“I want you to have the life you want too, Chase.”

“My job pays well, I’m not leaving it.”

“I’m not dumb Chase, I know you’ve been working for the Scavs.”

“You need to stop. I don’t want to see you get hurt… or worse. How do you think Jack feels about his father never being home?”

“It’s a little late for me to stop now Lia.” [tired] Chase said, sitting down on the couch, setting his food on the table in front of him.

“It’s never too late.”

“I’ve killed people, Lia.” [serious]

“I know, I’ve seen the blood on your clothes.”

“I’ve killed powerful people,” Chase said, “You can’t just erase that.”

“So we’ll move,” Lia replied, sitting down next to Chase, “We can leave Chroma and go to some other district, Helios is a big city.”

“You really think so?”

“I do, we can even start looking right now if you’d like.” Lia said, resting a hand on Chase’s shoulder.

“What will we do? For work I mean.”

“We’ll figure it out—corpo’s always hiring, you could use your expertise there. And I’m sure I can always make money with the dolls.”

“Please tell me you’re joking,” Chase said, “The last thing we need is you associating yourself with the dolls.”

“I’m just thinking of you and Jack, I’m willing to do whatever I can to support you.”

“Me too,” Chase said, “Maybe this is a conversation for tomorrow… I’ll stay home to figure it out.”

“Thank you, that means a lot to me.” [warmly] Lia said, pulling Chase into a hug.
