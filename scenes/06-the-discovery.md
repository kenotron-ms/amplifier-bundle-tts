# AUDIO PROFILE: Narrator — "Eight Years Old"
## The Discovery Scene

## THE SCENE: A Hidden Chamber Behind a Tiled Wall, Megabuilding, South Atlantic
Blaze has been here before — in his mind, at least. He's stood in enough
wrong rooms that he has a feel for them. This is a room that hides things.
He presses his hand to the tiled wall, feels for the hollow, and
the forearm cannon that lives where his arm used to be begins to glow.
What is behind the wall will change him in ways he cannot yet calculate.

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
Style: Grave and restrained. This is the most devastating scene in the story.
The narrator does not editorialise or sentimentalise — just reports, quietly,
the way you report something that cannot be made better by volume or poetry.
The rage when it comes should land like a door slamming in a silent house.
Pacing: Slow. Very slow. The discovery itself should have space around it.
Accent: Mature, measured — British-inflected American, slightly formal.

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Blaze has followed evidence to this room. His men searched it and found nothing.
Blaze found the hollow wall. What is inside is his daughter.
She was eight years old. This is the engine of everything that follows.

#### TRANSCRIPT
[mature, measured] The room smelled of blood.
Not the cloth — his men had already found the cloth.
This was different. More of it.

Blaze moved to the bathroom. More here, he said.
His men shifted uncomfortably. They had already searched the room.

[pause]

Then you weren't looking hard enough.

[slowly] He crossed to the leftmost tiled wall.
Dirt. Grime. A surface that said: nothing here, keep moving.
He pressed his palm flat against it. Felt for the mechanism. Nothing.
Then knocked — and heard what he was listening for.

[quietly] Hollow.

[a pause — the sound of men stepping back]

His arm unfolded. The cannon emerged from where his elbow had been,
cybernetics humming as they obeyed.
One smooth motion.
One very loud sound.

[a beat of silence after the blast]

[grave, quiet] The wall was gone.
Behind it: a small, damp chamber.
In a deep pool of blood lay his daughter.

[long pause]

Her limbs were missing. Her face was frozen.

Blaze clenched his teeth as something very hot filled his chest
and began to consume everything else.
His men took a step back.

[flat, eerily controlled] One of them leaned to another and whispered:
How old was she?

[bone-still] She was only eight years old.

[pause]

You said you found his base?

Yes, sir. Should I tell the others?

[turning to leave, carrying her]

Lock down the building.
Don't touch the Daemon.

[final, absolute]

He's all mine.
