# AUDIO PROFILE: Chase & Jack — "The Story of the Daemon"
## The Bedtime Story Scene

## THE SCENE: Jack's Bedroom, Later That Night
A child's bedroom in a neon city. Glow-in-the-dark stars on the ceiling, maybe.
A small bed. Jack tucked in now, the pink stuffed animal with the missing ear
pressed to his chest. Chase sits on the edge of the mattress and tells his son
a bedtime story — the story of a hero called Daemon who saved a little girl.
He doesn't mention that Daemon is him. He doesn't mention the girl.

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES
Style: Tender. Quietly aching. Chase is telling his son a version of the truth
he can live with — sanitized, heroic, the violence removed. The narrator
understands the irony without underlining it too heavily.
Chase's voice: Even, calm, a little weary — a dad doing the most human thing
he knows how to do.
Jack's voice: Half-asleep, enthusiastic, full of unspoiled wonder.
Pacing: Slow and warm. Jack's interruptions should feel spontaneous.
Accent: Neutral American — both of them.

### SAMPLE CONTEXT
Chase has just come back from work carrying the stuffed animal.
His wife wants him to leave the gang. His son doesn't know what the gang is.
This bedtime story is the only version of himself Chase can offer his child.

#### TRANSCRIPT
Chase: This story is about a very bad man. He stole and robbed and killed people.
But the worst thing he did — was have a daughter.
Jack: How is that a bad thing, dad?
Chase: Because he didn't love her. And tried to sell her to very bad people.
Jack: [excited] That's so mean! He's not a good dad like you!
Chase: [softly laughs] Yeah. He wasn't. But that's not where the story ends —
because this story has a hero.
Jack: [enthusiastically] Angel!
Chase: Yes. Angel. He found the girl, and her dad wasn't even there.
She was with her dad's coworkers. Daemon knew the girl was in danger — and saved her.
Jack: [yay] Yayy!
Chase: He brought her to a new house. A new family.
And in return... she gave him a pink stuffed animal dog. Even though it was missing an ear.
Jack: [whispering] That's like my one.
Chase: [smiling] The end. Time to go to bed.
I'll be here all day tomorrow — I can tell you stories the whole day.
